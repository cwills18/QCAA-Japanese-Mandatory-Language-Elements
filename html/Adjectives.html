Grammar and Sentence Structures

The following table summarises the mandatory grammar and sentence patterns used with adjectives.

Tenses in Polite and Plain Forms
面白いです。 大変です。
面白い。 大変だ。
It is interesting. It is awful.
_______________________________________________
面白いくないです。 大変じゃないです。
面白いくない。 大変じゃない。
It is not interesting. It is not awful.
_______________________________________________
面白いかったです。 大変でした。
面白いかった。 大変だった。
It was interesting. It was awful.
_______________________________________________
面白いくないかったです。 大変じゃないかったです。
面白いくないかった。 大変じゃないかった。
It was not interesting. It was not awful.
~ And ~
面白いくて有名です。 大変でつまらないです。
It's interesting and famous. It's awful and boring.
Comparisons
本を読むことより面白いです。 自転車に乗ることより大変です。
It's more interesting than reading a book. It's harder than riding a bike.
_______________________________________________
もっと面白いです。 もっと大変です。
It's more interesting. / It's funnier. It's more awful. / It's harder.
_______________________________________________
もっとも面白いです。 もっとも大変です。
It's the most interesting. / It's the funniest. It's the most awful. / It's the hardest.
_______________________________________________
一番面白いのは　日本語を勉強することです。 一番大変なのは　試験をおちることです。
The most interesting thing is learning Japanese. The most awful thing is failing a test.
Seems ~ (generic impression)
面白いそうです。 大変そうです。
It seems interesting. It seems awful.
Seems ~ (based on somewhat concrete information)
先生が忙しいようです。 宿題が大変なようです。
The teacher seems/appears busy. The homework seems to be hard.
(Maybe the teacher hasn't been replying to emails and is looking flustered) (Maybe I've had a look at the homework questions and quickly tried one)
Seems ~ (hearsay, based on less reliable information)
先生が忙しいらしいです。 宿題が大変らしいです。
I heard/Apparently/It seems the teacher is busy. I heard/Apparently/It seems the homework is hard.
Turning adjectives into adverbs
小さいく しずかに
finely ("small-ly"). quietly ("quiet-ly").
Turning adjectives into nouns
大きいさ べんりさ
size ("big-ness"). convenience ("convenient-ness").
