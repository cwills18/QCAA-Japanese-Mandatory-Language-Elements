Conjunctions

Conjunctions are words that are used to connect words, phrases and clauses. There are many conjunctions, but some common ones in English include: "and", "but", "or", "therefore" and "because". In Japanese, we have to be very careful to consider where the conjunction is being used, as there are different rules, and often entirely different words, for conjunctions depending on whether they appear at the start or the middle of a sentence.

These words can be used simply by adding them to the beginning of a sentence.

Common Conjunctions for Mid-Sentence

Using conjunctions in the middle of a sentence require a bit more care. Here is a sumary of the most common conjunctions. This list is not exhaustive. Conjunctions like "because", "if", "before" etc. will be covered in depth on other pages.

And
When the word before "and" is a true adjective (ignoreです) - use くて.
そのふでばこが安い ですくて買いたくなりました。
That pencilcase is cheap and I want to buy it.
(Literally: That pencilcase is cheap and it has become that I want to buy it.)
_______________________________________________
When the word before "and" is a quasi adjective or noun (ignoreです) - use で.
有名ですで行きたいです。
It's famous and I want to go there.
海ですで有名です。
It's a beach and it's famous.
_______________________________________________
When the word before "and" is a ます verb - use the て form.
行きますってみます。
I'll go and have a look.
朝ご飯を 食べますておさらを洗います。
I'll eat breakfast and wash the dishes.
But
For a polite version of "but", add が to the end of any polite sentence.
有名ですが、行きたくないです。
It's famous, but I don't want to go.
日本語があまり話せませんが、がんばります。
I can't really speak Japanese, but I'll try my best.
_______________________________________________
For a casual version of "but", add けど (or one of its variations such as けれど or けれども) to the end of any plain sentence.
おいしいけどけんこうによくない。
It's delicious but unhealthy.
見たけれど、つまらなかった。
I watched it but it was boring.
Or
Unfortunately, there are no hard and fast rules for "or" as it needs to be translated in many, many different ways depending on the sentence. But here are some general rules that may be helpful. Remember that if the "or" is being used comparatively (eg: "Which is faster, a lion or a tiger?"), you will need to use the separate rules for comparisons.

When giving two nouns as options, the most common way to express or is with the use of か.
来週の月曜日か火曜日まで宿題をします。
I will do the homework by Monday or Tuesday of next week.
バスか電車で行きます。
I'll go by bus or train.
_______________________________________________
When using "or" between two options, and especially with counters, often no "or" is needed - simply use both. In writing, mark with a comma or a ・.
５、６年前、日本を勉強し始めた。
I started studying Japanese 5 or 6 years ago.
かれは七つ・八つハンバーガーを食べてしまった！
He totally ate 7 or 8 hamburgers!
